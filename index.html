U.S. Troops Presence in Lebanon

A timeline and map show how United States’ military troop presence in Lebanon has shifted amid political tensions and where key military locations intersect with today’s regional conflicts.

By Lily Zuckerman

Amid ongoing tensions in the Middle East since nearly Israel’s founding in 1948, these visualizations emphasize the historical and present-day geopolitical significance of Lebanon in relation to the United States’ military. My first visualization is a timeline chart from 1950 to 2024 that demonstrates the fluctuating presence of U.S. army troops in Lebanon. The chart shows a surge in U.S. military presence in Lebanon during political instability and conflict. For example, the chart shows the highest number of U.S. troops in Lebanon in 1958, which makes sense given the U.S. Marine’s invasion of Lebanon that year. Then, we see another rise in the presence of U.S. troops in 1983, the same year of the Marine Barracks Bombing at Beirut, Lebanon. While data in recent years shows a relatively low U.S. military presence in Lebanon, tensions in the Middle East around territories and borders have continued to escalate. The interactive map contextualizes key locations to understand the fluctuating U.S. military presence over the years. For example, I highlight Beirut, where the Marine Barracks Bombing occurred in 1983, the Beirut–Rafic Hariri International Airport — where U.S. marines resided at their barracks when Israel invaded Lebanon and during the peacekeeping mission — alongside areas in southern Lebanon where recent evacuation warnings have been instructed amid cross-border conflicts between Hezbollah and Israel.
